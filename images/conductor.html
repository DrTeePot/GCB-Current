Conductor

The Guelph Concert Band is delighted to have two very talented conductors sharing our first concert on Nov 30: Chirs Cigolea and Dorothy Vreeswick-Kidd.

Chris Cigolea

Born in Romania, Chris's love of music was kindled at a young age playing violin under the instruction of his father, a prominent violinist of Focsani. He soon became interested in wind instruments and was performing on stage playing clarinet by the age of 12. At 14 Chris went to the Military School of Music in Bucharest, and found his principle instrument to this day, trumpet. He won the Romanian Youth National Competition three years in a row on trumpet when he was 17, 18 and 19.
Chris continued his studies at the National University of Music Bucharest. He was featured as a soloist with the Representative Military Music Orchestra of Romania and played with numerous radio and television orchestras and ensembles. In 1996 he moved to Canada and in 2001 received his Master of Music of Music in Performance from the University of Toronto. As a musician and conductor Chris has performed on stage with numerous other great musicians from all over the world. His repertoire ranges from jazz to classical, baroque to folk and also includes popular music. The majority of his professional experience has revolved around education and performance. Chris is the Principal Trumpet for the Guelph Symphony Orchestra, Trumpet instructor at University of Guelph and Music Director at Fern Hill School Oakville and Burlington. He was employed as a trumpet performer with Radio and Television Orchestra of Bucharest, The Representative Music of the Army, Brasov Symphony Orchestra and he served as a Teacher Assistant and Brass Chamber Music instructor at the University of Toronto assisting the Canadian Brass.
His performance and conducting is based on a wealth of information gathered from various artists and professors as Sergiu Celibidache, James Thompson, Arturo Sandoval and many others. Chris has studied conducting with Gillian McKay from University of Toronto, Paula Holcomb from State University of New York, Robert Ponto from University of Oregon and participated in Sergiu Celibidache’s Master Classes at The National University of Music in Bucharest Romania. In the summer of 2010 he was invited to be the Assistant Conductor for the European Opera Festival in France by Leonardo Gasparini, the former Paris Opera Conductor and Professor of Conducting at the Paris Conservatoire.

Dorothy Vreeswick-Kidd
