Our Conductor - Chris Cigolea

Born in Romania, Chris's love of music was kindled at a young age playing violin under the instruction of his father, a prominent violinist of Focsani. He soon became interested in wind instruments and was performing on stage playing clarinet by the age of 12. At 14 Chris went to the Military School of Music in Bucharest, and found his principle instrument to this day, trumpet. After studying the violin, clarinet and trumpet, Chris continued his studies at the National University of Music Bucharest, winning multiple national awards. He was featured as a soloist with the Representative Military Music Orchestra of Romania and played with numerous radio and television orchestras and ensembles. Chris moved to Canada in 1996 and in 2001 he received his Master of Music in Performance from the University of Toronto, where he is currently completing his Doctorate of Musical Arts in Performance.

As a musician and conductor Chris has recorded and performed on stage with numerous other great musicians from all over the world. His repertoire ranges from jazz to classical, baroque to folk and also includes popular music. The majority of his professional experience has revolved around education and performance.

Currently, Chris is the Conductor of the Guelph Youth Symphony Orchestra, Principal Trumpet for the Guelph Symphony Orchestra, Trumpet instructor at the University of Guelph and Music Director at St. John's Kilmarnock High School, among other positions. His trumpet solo album "Tribute To An Idol" was recognized in 2006 by the Juno Awards Committee as one of the best Solo Classical Albums in Canada.

He was employed as a trumpet performer with Radio and Television Orchestra of Bucharest, The Representative Music of the Army, Brasov Symphony Orchestra and served as a Teaching Assistant and Brass Chamber Music instructor at the University of Toronto assisting the Canadian Brass.

His performance and conducting is based on a wealth of information gathered from various artists and professors including James Thompson, Arturo Sandoval, Wynton Marsalis, Guy Few and many others. Chris has studied conducting with Gillian McKay at the University of Toronto, Paula Holcomb from the State University of New York and Robert Ponto from the University of Oregon as well as participating in Sergiu Celibidache’s Master Classes at The National University of Music in Bucharest Romania. In the summer of 2010 he was invited to be the Assistant Conductor for the European Opera Festival in France by Leonardo Gasparini, the former Paris Opera Conductor and Professor of Conducting at the Paris Conservatoire.
